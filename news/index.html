2025-02-06 - Our lab's project template is now open source!

We're excited to share that we've open-sourced our project template, designed to make starting new projects a breeze and homogeneous across all our projects.

This template uses Cookiecutter, a widely-used tool that helps create projects from templates.

Our template is flexible, letting you choose options like project type (research or software), package management (uv, pip, conda), and your preferred code editors we most use (Sublime Text or VSCode).

By sharing we hope to help others in the scientific community organize their projects effectively and also motivate others to share their ways of structuring research and software projects.

Check it out and suggest improvements on GitHub: https://github.com/rendeirolab/_project_template

2025-01-27 - Our lab manual is now open source!

We are excited to announce that the Rendeiro Lab Manual is now open source and accessible to all.

This comprehensive guide details our lab's culture, procedures, and workflows, aiming to foster a collaborative and efficient research environment. You can explore the manual on our website: https://rendeiro.group/lab-manual

Our decision to make this manual open source was inspired by the generosity of other labs that have shared their resources. In particular, we acknowledge the Aly Lab and the Getz Lab for their open-source manuals, which served as valuable references in developing our own.

We envision this manual as a living document, evolving with contributions from the broader scientific community. By sharing our practices and protocols, we hope to promote transparency and collaboration in research. We invite you to explore, utilize, and contribute to the manual, ensuring it remains a dynamic and valuable resource for all.

The manual is hosted on GitHub, where you can access the source code and even propose changes: https://github.com/rendeirolab/lab-manual

We are curious on the reaction and feedback from the research community, please reach out to us.

2024-11-16 - Our manuscript on the prediction of biological age from histopathological insights is now online!

Our study reveals focuses on the structural changes human tissues undergo during aging and how they reflect its physiological state.

By analyzing over 25,000 histopathological images across various tissues from nearly 1,000 individuals, we developed 'tissue clocks' that can estimate biological age based on tissue morphological characteristics. We found that aging manifests differently in various organs, some showing accelerated changes early in life. Conversely, some individuals show markedly more accceleated aging than others, sometimes in a organ-specific manner. Leveraging these tissue-specific and highly physiological readouts of aging, we demonstrate how we can derive the a proxy for tissue-specific, morphologically driven biological age from blood samples and validate them in diseased samples.

This study highlights the intricate relationship between tissue health and biological aging, and could revolutionize the way we assess and understand the biological processes leading to aging-related diseases.

Read the full work here: https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.11.14.618081
